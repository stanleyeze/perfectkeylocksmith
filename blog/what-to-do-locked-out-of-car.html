What to Do When You're Locked Out of Your Car

Getting locked out of your car is one of the most frustrating experiences you can have. Whether you left your keys on the seat, locked them in the trunk, or your key fob battery died, you need a plan. Step-by-Step Guide to Handling a Car Lockout

1

Stay Calm and Assess the Situation

Take a deep breath. Panicking won't help and could lead to poor decisions like trying to break a window. First, check all doors‚Äîsometimes one might be unlocked. Also check if you have a spare key at home or with a family member nearby.

2

Check for a Spare Key

Do you have a spare key at home? Many people keep a spare with a trusted friend or family member. Some newer vehicles also have a valet key in the owner's manual. If someone can bring you a spare, this is the fastest free solution.

3

Contact Your Roadside Assistance

If you have CAA, your car insurance roadside assistance, or a dealership membership, check if lockout service is covered. Many plans include free lockout assistance. However, wait times can be 1-2 hours during busy periods.

4

Call a Professional Locksmith

For the fastest response (usually 30 minutes or less), call a local locksmith. Professional locksmiths have specialized tools to unlock any vehicle without causing damage. Make sure to get a price estimate before they arrive.

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Don't Try These DIY Methods

Avoid using coat hangers, slim jims, or wedges from YouTube videos. Modern cars have complex lock mechanisms and airbag sensors in the doors. DIY attempts often cause:

  • Scratched paint and window tint
  • Damaged door seals and weather stripping
  • Broken lock mechanisms (costing $200-500 to repair)
  • Triggered airbag sensors

Emergency Situations: When to Act Fast

üö® Call 911 Immediately If:

  • A child or pet is locked inside the vehicle
  • It's extremely hot or cold outside
  • You're in an unsafe location
  • Someone inside the car needs medical attention

In life-threatening situations, breaking a window is justified. Use something hard to strike the corner of a rear window (not the front windshield).

How Much Does Car Lockout Service Cost?

In Ontario, professional car lockout services typically cost:

  • Standard hours (8am-8pm): $75-$120
  • After hours/weekends: $100-$150
  • Holidays: $120-$180

These prices usually include the service call and the unlock. Beware of locksmiths who quote very low prices on the phone, then add fees when they arrive.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take a locksmith to unlock a car?

A professional locksmith can typically unlock a car in 5-15 minutes depending on the vehicle's lock system. Modern cars with advanced security systems may take slightly longer, but rarely more than 20 minutes.

Will a locksmith damage my car when unlocking it?

No. Professional locksmiths use specialized tools designed to unlock vehicles without causing any damage. Unlike DIY methods, professional tools won't scratch your paint, damage door seals, or harm internal mechanisms.

Can a locksmith make a new car key on the spot?

Yes! Most mobile locksmiths can cut and program new car keys at your location. This includes transponder keys and key fobs for most vehicle makes and models.
